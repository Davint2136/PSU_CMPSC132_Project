This project is a single-player, terminal-based number guessing game. All you need to play is your brain, a screen, and a keyboard!

HOW TO PLAY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The computer randomly generates a number between 1 and 100 (inclusive), and the player has 7 tries to guess it. After each guess, the computer will give feedback, telling the player if their guess is too high, too low, or correct. After each game, the correct number and number of attempts are saved to the player's history. At the end of each game, the player is prompted to decide whether or not they wish to play again. Should they choose not to, the player's history and gameplay statistics are displayed. These statistics describe the number of games played, the player's best score (i.e. smallest number of attempts), and the player's game-by-game history.

REGARDING GUESSES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Guesses must be an integer. If they aren't integers, the game will prompt the player to guess again. No penalties for invalid guesses are applied. Guesses less than 0 and greater than 100 are possible, but are heavily discouraged, as they will always be outside the range of the random number generator.
